Residential housing

Low-Density Residential

Peaceful, spacious homes surrounded by nature. Ideal for families and Elders. Features gardens, traditional architecture, and sustainability. Lower-density housing at Naawi-Oodena is designed to reflect traditional values of community, family, and connection to the land. These homes offer peaceful, spacious environments ideal for families, Elders, and multi-generational households.

Medium-Density Residential

Low-rise apartments with shared green space and cultural design. Promotes affordable, inclusive community living for all ages. Medium-density housing helps support a growing and diverse population while promoting affordability, inclusivity, and community connection.




Commercial Mixed-Use

The Commercial Mixed-Use district at Naawi-Oodena will be a vibrant hub where business, culture, and everyday life intersect. It supports local entrepreneurship and walkable living environments. This district supports economic self-determination, encouraging Indigenous entrepreneurship while offering residents everyday amenities within walking distance. It's a place where culture and commerce come together.





Community areas

Naawi-Oodena’s community spaces serve as the heart of connection, culture, and collective wellness. These spaces are for gathering, sharing, healing, and growing together. These spaces are built with Elder and youth guidance, reflecting traditional values and creating a safe, inclusive environment for people of all backgrounds to come together in unity.




Sports and Recreation

Sports and recreation are essential for well-being, youth engagement, and holistic development at Naawi-Oodena. These facilities will support both traditional and contemporary forms of activity. Inspired by the Four Directions of wellness — physical, mental, emotional, and spiritual — Naawi-Oodena’s sports and recreation areas promote healthy living while respecting the land and traditions of Indigenous communities.




Cultural Campus and Education

Naawi-Oodena’s cultural and education district will focus on restoring Indigenous knowledge systems, language revitalization, and accessible lifelong learning. The Cultural and Education Campus is a place where knowledge flows between Elders and youth, between tradition and innovation, and where every student — child or adult — can find pride and purpose in Indigenous identity and achievement.




Mixed-Use Village

The Mixed-Use Village is the beating heart of social and creative life at Naawi-Oodena. It blends cultural expression, economic opportunity, and modern living in a dynamic, community-led environment. The Mixed-Use Village is a place of celebration and self-determination, where innovation meets tradition, and where everyday life is infused with culture, creativity, and community pride.
